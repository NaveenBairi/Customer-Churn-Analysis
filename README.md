This Power BI project focuses on analyzing telecom customer data to understand customer churn behavior.
It explores customer demographics, account details, service usage, and billing information.
The project aims to identify key factors that influence why customers leave the service.
Data cleaning and preprocessing were performed to ensure accurate analysis.
DAX measures were created to calculate churn rates and customer segments.
Interactive dashboards visualize churn trends across tenure, charges, and customer types.
The analysis highlights high-risk customer segments prone to churn.
Insights help in understanding the relationship between pricing and customer retention.
The dashboard supports data-driven decision-making for retention strategies.
Overall, the project demonstrates the effective use of Power BI for business analysis.
key Insights:
--New customers (0–12 months) show higher churn, while long-term customers are more stable.
Senior citizens show higher churn percentage  compared  to Non-senior customers are more likely to stay.
Monthly charges increase with tenure where Male and female charges follow a similar pattern
--
--Fiber optic customers have the highest churn rate, while customers with multiple services churn less.
Month-to-month + Fiber optic customers have the highest churn combination, whereas long-term contracts reduce churn across all services.
Phone service alone does not significantly drive churn, as most customers already subscribe to it.--
--Month-to-month contract customers have the highest churn rate, while long-term contracts (1-year and 2-year) show much better retention.
Customers using electronic check as a payment method churn significantly more than those using other payment options.
Customers with short tenure (0–12 months) are more likely to churn compared to long-tenure customers--
